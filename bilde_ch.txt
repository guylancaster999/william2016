Start Wythenshawe Park Wythenshawe Road M23 0AB. The ride quickly meanders through the suburban setting before opening up onto the quiet country lanes through Ashely. The trees overhang us as we cycle to Tatton Park and take advantage of the cycle route through the gorgeous park itself. Great views of the scenery and wildlife, deer can be spotted enjoying the sanctuary, before we exit south of the park and cycle east. The roads meander, slinking their way through quaint settlements until we reach the truly picturesque village of Great Budworth.From Great Budworth it's a short cycle through Comberbach before entering our designated rest stop, the Anderton Boat Lift. Built in 1875 the 50ft vertical boat lift connects the River Weaver and the Trent Mersey Canal. Here we can enjoy the café and picnic tables and watch the lift in action.The return route takes us back through Great Budworth then we cycle north, taking advantage of the quiet lanes towards High Legh then onto Dunham Massey. Another potential stop to take in the wonderful scenery at Dunham Massey before we cycle back into suburbia and back to Wythneshawe Park. <td><a href="https://www.facebook.com/CriticalMassManchester"><strong>Critical Mass</strong></a>

<a href="https://www.facebook.com/CriticalMassManchester"><img align="right" src="http://chorltonwanderers.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/criticalmass1.gif" alt="criticalmass1" width="150" height="210" align="right" /></a>
Critical Mass is a bike ride that happens in our city and cities all over the world every last Friday of the month. Critical Mass rides began in San Francisco in 1992 and rides spread around the United States and the world with over 200 rides taking place in 2006. Critical Mass has no leaders, and no central organization licenses rides. In every city that has a Critical Mass ride, some locals simply picked a date, time, and location for the ride and publicised it, and thus the ride was born. Critical Mass is an idea and an event, not an organization. It is a great way to meet other cyclists and to have a fun bike ride. It is not a fast ride and often children come too, everyone is welcome to come and celebrate the bicycle! Meet central library, st peters square, Manchester.</td>

Critical Mass is an idea and an event, not an organization. It is a great way to meet other cyclists and to have a fun bike ride. It is not a fast ride and often children come too, everyone is welcome to come and celebrate the bicycle! Meet central library, st peters square, Manchester.</td>
